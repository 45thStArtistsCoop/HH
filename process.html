An Overview of the Process

Phase 1

Identify the options

Why it matters

It's important for H+H members to know that all the options are on the table before we begin educating, discussing, and voting. Getting member input early in the process will insure that all members feel heard and can participate in surfacing ideas.

How we'll do it

On June 8th, Stevo circulated an email outlining the proposed process. In that email was a link to a form that asked individual members to initially prioritize the major projects facing H+H and to list other projects they wanted to learn more about.

Presenting the results

Stevo will present the results of the membe survey at the next volunteer meeting of interested H+H members on June 24th. He will also circulate the results of the member survey on June 25th for those members who cannot attend the meeting, as well as posting them on the website.

Making sure it's fair

Input to the survey will open for 2 weeks to all H+H members and will include open comments for each item. New options and ideas will be noted at the meeting on June 24th and added to the survey before distribution on the 25th.

How long will it take?

There is already a meeting scheduled for June 24th and at least 1 additional meeting may be ncessary to plan for Phase 2.

Hopefully complete by July 30th.

Phase 2

Educate members

Why it matters

It's important for H+H members to know as much as possible about as many of the options as possible before we discuss them or vote on them. H+H members present at the initial meeting in May highlighted education as the top priority of this decision-making process.

How we'll do it

After the presentions of the survey results from Phase 1, H+H members present at the June 24th meeting will discuss how they would like to learn about the projects, options, and other ideas. There are many ways to create, collecte and organize education materials on the various ideas that are surfaced in Phase 1, and we hope to draw up plans for executing Phase 2 at the June 24th meeting.

Making sure it's fair

If there is no concensus at the June 24th meeting for how to fairly educate H+H members on the ideas and options surfaced in Phase 1, more discussion and a vote might be necessary.

How long will it take?

This could potentially be the longest phase and it will be entirely dependent on the process for educating members outlined at the end of Phase 1.

Hopefully complete by December 31st, 2018.

Phase 3

Discuss Options

Why it matters

It's important for H+H members to hear as many of their fellow members' opinions as possible before voting on the ideas and options we will all learn about in Phase 2. This will insure that members feel heard and informed.

How we'll do it

After the education materials generated in Phase 2 are distributed, H+H members will be responsible for opening conversations with their fellow members as much as possible. In addition, additional meetings might be necessary for those that wish to attend and discuss the options in person.

Making sure it's fair

Discourse can be logistically and emotionally difficuly, so it behooves H+H members to be as inclusive and kind as possible as they learn and advocate in Phase 3. We will also dicuss ways to make this process more inclusive at the conclusion of Phase 2.

How long will it take?

Too hard to tell at the moment. Stevo will update this section after the end of Phase 1 and Phase 2.

To be determined.

Phase 4

Voting on the options

Why it matters

It's important for H+H members to collectively generate an agreed-upon budget for the accumulating difference between rents and expenditures. This will hopefully include a list of prioritized projects based on education materials created in Phase 2.

How we'll do it

After discussing the projects and ideas from Phase 2, H+H members will need to decide on a voting process. This process will need to be agreed upon in advance of any general vote, so this will likely take the form of a meeting to discuss the voting process, with interested members volunteering to attend and distribute information on that process to the members that cannot attend.

Making sure it's fair

Voting will be the most important part of this process with the most care taken to insure that all members feel like they were a part of it. As Phase 3 winds down, we will likely need to solicit more ideas from H+H members for how to keep a final vote fair.

How long will it take?

Too hard to tell at the moment. Stevo will update this section after the end of Phase 1 and Phase 2, and Phase 2.

To be determined.
